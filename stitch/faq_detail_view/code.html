Password reset email not received

Confidence Score
92%
Case Count 18 instances

Problem Statement

Users are reporting that after initiating a password reset request via the login portal, the expected recovery email never arrives in their primary inbox, preventing account access.

Cause Explanation

Our analysis indicates that 85% of these cases are due to aggressive SMTP filtering on legacy corporate domains (e.g., .edu or .org) which flag our automated sender address as bulk mail. Secondary causes include account-level blocklists or typos in the initial registration phase.

Step-by-step fix

1

Check the Spam, Junk, or Promotions folders specifically. Many filters reroute automated emails without notifying the user.

2

Whitelist no-reply@system.knowledge.base in your email provider's settings to ensure future delivery.

3

Wait at least 10 minutes. High server load on legacy domains can sometimes delay delivery beyond the usual 30-second window.

4

Re-attempt the reset request only once. Multiple rapid requests may trigger a security lock on the account for 24 hours.

Edge Cases

  • SSO-enabled accounts (Google/GitHub)
  • Bounced emails due to mailbox full
  • Dormant accounts marked for deletion

When to contact support

If the user has verified the email is not in spam and has waited 30 minutes, escalate to Tier 2 to check SMTP logs for specific bounce codes.
